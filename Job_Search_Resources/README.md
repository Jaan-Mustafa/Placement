## LinkedIn Tricks — Know About Hiring the Moment It Happens

### 1. Turn On Notifications for Specific People (Most Important)
- Go to the profile of a **recruiter or senior engineer** at your target company
- Click the **bell icon** next to the Connect/Follow button
- Select **"All new posts"**
- You'll get a push notification the second they post "we're hiring" or "DM me"
- Do this for 20–30 key people across target companies — this alone beats everything else

### 2. Follow Recruiters Directly (Not Just the Company Page)
- Search: `"recruiter" "Google"` or `"talent acquisition" "Amazon"` in LinkedIn search
- Filter by **People** → **1st & 2nd degree connections**
- Follow them (you don't need to connect) — their posts appear in your feed
- Company pages post jobs late; **recruiters post them first**

### 3. Sort Your Feed by "Recent" Not "Top"
- On LinkedIn feed, click the **sort icon** (top right of feed) → switch to **"Recent"**
- LinkedIn's default "Top" algorithm buries fresh posts
- "Recent" shows posts in real time — hiring posts appear within minutes of being published

### 4. Saved Search + Job Alert (Set It Up Right)
- Go to LinkedIn Jobs → search your role + location
- Click **"Set Alert"** → choose **Daily** or **Weekly**
- Also save the search — LinkedIn will email you new matches the moment they're indexed
- Use **Boolean filters**: `"Software Engineer" AND ("Bangalore" OR "Remote") NOT "10+ years"`

### 5. LinkedIn Post Search Hack (Find Hiring Posts Live)
Go to LinkedIn Search → filter by **Posts** → sort by **Latest** → use these searches daily:

**The problem with just `"SDE" AND "hiring"`:**
Recruiters and engineers write posts differently — some say "Software Engineer", some say "SWE", "dev", "backend", "full stack". You miss ~70% of posts by searching only one term.

**Fix — use OR to cover every variation of the role:**

```
("SDE" OR "software engineer" OR "SWE" OR "software developer" OR "backend engineer" OR "frontend engineer" OR "full stack developer") AND ("hiring" OR "we're hiring" OR "looking for" OR "openings" OR "DM me")
```

**Shorter version that works well:**
```
("SDE" OR "software engineer" OR "SWE" OR "developer") AND ("hiring" OR "openings" OR "DM me" OR "looking for")
```

**For India / Bangalore specifically:**
```
("software engineer" OR "SDE" OR "SWE" OR "developer") AND ("hiring" OR "openings") AND ("bangalore" OR "india" OR "remote")
```

**For fresher / entry level:**
```
("fresher" OR "new grad" OR "entry level" OR "0-2 years" OR "campus") AND ("hiring" OR "openings" OR "looking for") AND ("engineer" OR "developer" OR "SDE")
```

**For catching referral posts (high value):**
```
("referral" OR "refer") AND ("engineer" OR "SDE" OR "developer") AND ("hiring" OR "openings" OR "dm me" OR "ping me")
```

**For GCC / Dubai:**
```
("software engineer" OR "SDE" OR "developer") AND ("hiring" OR "openings") AND ("dubai" OR "UAE" OR "gulf" OR "riyadh" OR "qatar")
```

**Pro tips for LinkedIn post search:**
- Always sort by **Latest** not Relevance — you want real-time posts
- Run these searches **Monday and Tuesday mornings** (peak hiring post time)
- Save each search as a **bookmark** in your browser — open all tabs together daily
- When you find a post, **comment within 30 minutes** — early commenters get noticed by the recruiter

### 6. Google Site Search for LinkedIn Hiring Posts
- Use Google to search LinkedIn posts that LinkedIn's own search misses:
  ```
  site:linkedin.com/posts "we are hiring" "software engineer" "bangalore" 2024
  ```
  ```
  site:linkedin.com "my team is hiring" "react" OR "java" -intitle:"jobs"
  ```
- Google indexes LinkedIn posts faster than LinkedIn's own search sometimes

### 7. Turn On Company Page Notifications
- Go to any target company's LinkedIn page
- Click the **bell icon** on the page → **"All new posts"**
- You'll be notified when the company officially posts roles or shares hiring updates

### 8. Engage Early = More Visibility
- When you see a "we're hiring" post — **comment immediately** (within 30 mins)
- Early engagement pushes the post to more people's feeds AND the recruiter sees your name
- Don't just say "interested" — write something specific: *"I've been working with X and would love to connect"*

### 9. LinkedIn Premium — Worth It for Job Search
- **InMail** — message recruiters directly without being connected
- **"Who viewed your profile"** — reach out to recruiters who viewed you
- **Applicant insights** — see how you compare to other applicants before applying
- **Open Profile** — anyone can message you for free (increases inbound from recruiters)

### 10. Set Up Google Alerts as a Backup
- Go to [google.com/alerts](https://www.google.com/alerts)
- Create alerts for:
  - `"Company Name" hiring 2024`
  - `"Company Name" careers software engineer`
  - `"Company Name" openings`
- Delivered to your inbox daily — catches news articles, blog posts, and LinkedIn posts Google indexes

### 11. Use "Open to Work" Strategically
- Turn on **"Open to Work"** visible to **recruiters only** (not public) to avoid awkwardness at current job
- Fill in: roles, locations (include Remote), job types, and start date (Immediately)
- LinkedIn actively surfaces your profile to recruiters running candidate searches

### 12. Best Time to Check LinkedIn
| Day | Why |
|---|---|
| **Monday 9–11 AM** | Recruiters post new openings at week start |
| **Tuesday–Wednesday** | Peak hiring post activity |
| **Avoid Friday PM** | Slowest time for new postings |

## Cold Outreach & Referral Strategy

1. **Find recruiters on LinkedIn** — search `"recruiter" + "company name"`
2. **Email format**: `firstname.lastname@company.com` (verify via Hunter.io)
3. **Hunter.io** — find professional email addresses
4. **Rocketreach** — email + contact lookup for professionals
5. **Alumni network** — reach out via LinkedIn or college portal for referrals

## Quick Checklist Before Applying

- [ ] Resume is ATS-friendly (no tables/images in text layer)
- [ ] Resume tailored to the specific JD keywords
- [ ] LinkedIn profile is updated and open to work
- [ ] GitHub has pinned, documented projects
- [ ] Cover letter ready (role-specific, not generic)
- [ ] Referral sourced if possible
- [ ] Company researched (product, recent news, culture)
